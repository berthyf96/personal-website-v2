Berthy Feng

Hi! I'm a senior at Princeton University, majoring in Computer Science with a certificate in Statistics & Machine Learning.

Most recently, I was a Summer 2018 SWE Intern at Google. I worked on the Photos Machine Intelligence team in the LA office, where I developed a data pipeline for models related to people clustering.

I'm primarily interested in deep learning and its applications in audio and image processing. In Spring 2018, I completed an independent research project on Hierarchical Recurrent Neural Networks for Audio Super-Resolution. I built a model for improving the quality of digital audio without any inputs other than the low-quality input waveform. I also proposed ways of improving the model, including through perceptual losses and generative adversarial networks (GANs). My project won the Princeton CS Department's Best Poster Award.

Other related experiences:

  • Deep learning for bandwidth expansion using perceptual loss, a research project in conjunction with Prof. Adam Finkelstein (Princeton), Zeyu Jin (Adobe Research), and Jiaqi Su (Princeton).
  • Analyzing the effect of different text encodings on visual question answering (VQA) models.
  • Marketing Director for HackPrinceton, a semi-annual hackathon.
  • Developer of Pixplor, an iOS app for nursing-home residents.

Only slightly related experience: I produce music, mostly electronic, using a mix of audio recording and digital/analog synthesis. I play piano and am slowly learning guitar.
